About KTZ

JSC “NC“ KTZ ”: A Brief History & Profile
Infrastructure Backbone:** Originating from early 20th-century lines like the 1904 Orenburg-Tashkent highway, railways have grown into Kazakhstan's vital socio-economic backbone.
National Impact:** Today, the railway network employs approximately 1% of the country’s population, sustaining multi-generational worker dynasties and connecting all domestic regions to international trade.
Network Expansion:** Throughout the 20th century, critical developments like the Turkestan-Siberian (Turksib) railway and 1960s electrification projects vastly expanded the nation's transit capacity.
Global Integration:** Major modern milestones include joining networks with China at the Druzhba-Alashankou crossing in 1990 and establishing the international "Uzen-Bereket-Gorgan" corridor in 2014.
Modern Evolution:** Officially established as a national corporation in 2002, JSC “NC“ KTZ ” has successfully transitioned from a traditional railway operator into a premier transport and logistics holding.
Strategic Vision:** The holding now drives Kazakhstan's transit potential by integrating global infrastructure and executing key state initiatives like the "Nurly Zhol" program.
